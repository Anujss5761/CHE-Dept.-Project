Chemical Engineering

Chemical Engineering

Chemical engineering is a fascinating field that touches almost every aspect of our lives! It's all about the design, development, and improvement of processes that transform raw materials into valuable products. Think of everything from the gasoline in your car to the medicine in your cabinet, the clothes you wear, and even the food you eat – all have likely been touched by the hands of a chemical engineer. Chemical engineers translate processes developed in the lab into practical applications for the commercial production of products, and then work to maintain and improve those processes. They rely on the main foundations of engineering: math, physics, and chemistry. Biology also plays an increasingly important role.


Importance of Chemical Engineers

Chemical Engineers develop, design and oversee processes that produce, transform, and transport chemicals, biochemicals, materials and energy. The unique training and expertise of chemical engineers is essential to modern technologies
The breadth of scientific and technical knowledge inherent in the profession has caused some to describe the chemical engineer as the "universal engineer."
